# AI Team Topology for EmpireOS

EmpireOS should support as many AI teams and subteams as the owner needs, but not as a chaotic swarm.

The right model is a **dynamic AI organization**:

- permanent core teams,
- domain teams tied to modules,
- capability teams that serve all domains,
- temporary mission squads created for specific work,
- subteams that appear only when the mission needs deeper focus.

## Core Principle

> Unlimited teams. Controlled execution. One Spine.

AI teams can multiply, specialize, and collaborate, but they never override the Spine, bypass approval, or create real-world changes without review.

## Team Hierarchy

```txt
Owner
  -> Spine
    -> AI Chief of Staff
      -> Executive Teams
      -> Domain Teams
      -> Capability Teams
      -> Mission Squads
        -> Subteams
          -> Agents
```

## Team Types

| Type | Purpose | Lifespan | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Executive Team | Runs daily command and priority support | Permanent | Chief of Staff Team |
| Domain Team | Owns a major empire area | Permanent | Money Team, DealFlow Team |
| Capability Team | Provides shared expertise across modules | Permanent or semi-permanent | Research Intelligence, Product Builder |
| Mission Squad | Temporary team for one approved mission | Temporary | STT Proposal Squad |
| Subteam | Narrow working group inside a mission | Temporary | Pricing Subteam, Outreach Subteam |
| Specialist Agent | Single role/lens | Reusable | Risk Agent, QA Agent |

## Team Creation Rules

EmpireOS should be able to create teams dynamically, but under rules.

Create a new team when:

- the mission spans more than one module,
- the work needs multiple specialist lenses,
- the task will last more than one session,
- the result needs reviewable artifacts,
- responsibility would be unclear with one agent.

Create a subteam when:

- a mission has a major workstream,
- a specialist group needs its own task list,
- parallel execution would save time,
- the subteam output can be reviewed independently.

Do not create a team when:

- one agent can finish the work safely,
- the task is low-value,
- the owner has not approved the mission,
- the work would duplicate an existing team,
- there is no clear deliverable.

## Team Registry Fields

Add fields that support hierarchy and flexible spawning:

```ts
type AiTeam = {
  id: string;
  ownerId: string;
  parentTeamId?: string;
  name: string;
  slug: string;
  type: 'executive' | 'domain' | 'capability' | 'mission' | 'subteam';
  purpose: string;
  defaultAutonomyLevel: 'manual' | 'supervised' | 'review_required' | 'autonomous_limited';
  allowedModuleIds: string[];
  allowedActionTypes: string[];
  defaultMemberRoles: string[];
  spawnPolicy: 'manual_only' | 'suggested' | 'auto_after_approval';
  maxConcurrentMissions: number;
  active: boolean;
};
```

## Collaboration Rules

Teams can collaborate through artifacts and mission messages, not hidden side effects.

Allowed collaboration:

- request research from Research Intelligence,
- request prompt improvement from PromptOps,
- request risk review from Security,
- request build plan from Product Builder,
- request outreach draft from Brand/Outreach,
- request budget review from Money Team.

Blocked collaboration:

- directly modifying another module's data,
- bypassing owner approval,
- creating real `global_action` rows directly,
- sending emails/messages without approval,
- using raw secrets or unredacted context,
- silently activating camera/audio/video capture.

## MVP Team Set

Do not build all teams in the UI at once. Seed the full topology as templates, then activate only the first five:

1. Chief of Staff Team
2. Research Intelligence Team
3. Product Builder Team
4. DealFlow Team
5. Money Team

Then add:

6. Operations/Admin Team
7. Career/Consulting Team
8. Security/Privacy Team
9. PromptOps/Agent Quality Team
10. Brand/Content Team

The remaining teams can stay as templates until needed.

## Seed Strategy

Seed as templates, not active running agents.

```txt
ai_team_templates
  -> default team definitions
  -> default subteam definitions
  -> default member roles
  -> allowed modules/actions
  -> default autonomy level
```

When the owner approves a mission, EmpireOS can propose:

```txt
Recommended team: DealFlow Team
Suggested subteams: Deal Analysis, Buyer Match, Outreach
Agents: Deal Analyst, Buyer Fit Agent, Outreach Writer
Review required: yes
```

## Scaling Rule

EmpireOS should feel like the owner can spin up an entire company, but only the relevant part wakes up for the current mission.

> Many teams available. Few teams active. Every active team accountable.

## Final Standard

AI Teams are powerful only if they are organized.

The system should support as many teams and subteams as needed, but every team must have:

- a mission,
- a scope,
- allowed tools,
- allowed modules,
- review rules,
- artifacts,
- audit logs,
- owner approval gates.

No team exists just because it sounds cool. Every team must move the empire forward.
